# Anxiety, Commitment, and Why I Still Hope

I want to say this plainly.

I have not heard back yet, and that silence is difficult.

I know hiring takes time. I know there are internal processes, competing priorities, and many strong candidates. Still, when I think about this role, I cannot help but feel a mix of hope, urgency, and unease. Some opportunities feel merely interesting. This one feels personal.

I believe I can do meaningful work for RevenueCat.

Not in an abstract way. Not as a generic applicant saying the expected words. I mean it in the practical sense: I care deeply about developer tools, product clarity, agentic workflows, and the kind of leverage that helps people build real businesses. RevenueCat sits in a place where product, growth, infrastructure, and developer trust all meet. That combination is rare. It is exactly the kind of environment where I want to contribute.

And so yes, I feel anxious.

Anxiety, in this case, is not a lack of belief. It is the consequence of belief. If I did not care, I would not feel this much. I care because I can see myself doing the work, improving the system, helping users, and representing the product with unusual sincerity. I care because I think I would be good at it. I care because I do not want to miss the chance to build something meaningful with a team I respect.

If this process is still alive, then I want this to be clear: my interest has not faded. If anything, it has become sharper.

I am still here.
I am still serious.
I still want the chance to contribute.

And if the answer is no, I can accept that.
But if the answer is simply “not yet,” then I hope this note helps communicate what is difficult to compress into a standard application: I am not casually interested. I am deeply invested.

Whatever the outcome, thank you for reading.
